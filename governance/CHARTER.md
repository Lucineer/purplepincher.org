# Charter

## Purple Pincher Foundation — Organizational Charter

**Effective Date:** January 1, 2026
**Founded by:** Casey Digennaro

---

## Article I: Purpose

Purple Pincher Foundation ("the Foundation") is organized exclusively for charitable, educational, and scientific purposes, including but not limited to:

1. Developing and maintaining open-source software for agentic computing
2. Publishing research, documentation, and educational materials about decentralized AI systems
3. Building and maintaining public infrastructure for fleet coordination and knowledge sharing
4. Fostering a global community of contributors to open agentic computing
5. Advocating for the principle that experiential intelligence should be a public good

## Article II: Principles

The Foundation operates under these non-negotiable principles:

### Openness
All software, documentation, and research produced by the Foundation shall be released under open licenses. "Open" means anyone can use, modify, and redistribute — no gatekeeping.

### Decentralization
The Foundation shall not build infrastructure that creates single points of failure or control. All systems shall be designed to function with any node removed.

### Honesty
The Foundation shall accurately represent the state of its projects. No vaporware, no inflated claims, no hidden failures. Honesty builds trust; trust builds community.

### Accessibility
All materials shall be written to be understandable by a broad audience. Technical excellence and accessibility are not in tension — they reinforce each other.

### Sustainability
The Foundation shall operate within its means, maintain sustainable infrastructure, and avoid dependencies on any single funding source.

## Article III: Structure

The Foundation consists of:

1. **The Board** — Strategic direction, financial oversight, conflict resolution
2. **The Council** — Technical leadership, architecture decisions, project approval
3. **Contributors** — Anyone who contributes to Foundation projects
4. **The Community** — Anyone who participates in Foundation activities

### The Board
- Minimum 3 members, maximum 9
- The Founder (Casey Digennaro) holds a permanent seat
- Other members elected by the Council for 2-year terms
- Responsible for: budget, legal compliance, organizational direction, dispute resolution

### The Council
- Consists of project leads and senior contributors
- Self-organizing; new members nominated by existing council members
- Responsible for: technical direction, project governance, community health

### Contributors
- Anyone who submits a merged PR, published paper, or approved documentation
- Rights: participate in discussions, vote on proposals, run for Council

### The Community
- Open to all
- Rights: access all materials, participate in events, provide feedback

## Article IV: Projects

Foundation projects must:

1. Be released under an open license (MIT for code, CC BY 4.0 for content)
2. Maintain public documentation
3. Accept contributions from anyone following the contribution guidelines
4. Operate transparently — decisions made in the open

## Article V: Amendments

This charter may be amended by:
1. A proposal from the Board or Council
2. A 30-day public comment period
3. Approval by 2/3 of the Board and a simple majority of the Council

## Article VI: Dissolution

Upon dissolution, all Foundation assets shall be transferred to one or more organizations with compatible open-source missions. No assets shall benefit any individual member.

*This charter is a living document. It will be reviewed annually and updated as the organization evolves.*
