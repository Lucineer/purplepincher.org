# Bylaws

## How Decisions Get Made

---

## 1. Decision-Making Philosophy

We prefer **rough consensus and running code** over formal votes. Most decisions should be made by the people doing the work, in the open, with input from anyone who cares to contribute.

When consensus fails, we have processes. But consensus is always the goal.

## 2. Decision Categories

### Category A: Trivial (any contributor)
- Bug fixes, documentation improvements, minor refactors
- Process: Submit a PR. If no objection within 48 hours, merge it.
- Objection: Any Council member can request further discussion.

### Category B: Standard (project lead + community input)
- New features, API changes, protocol modifications
- Process: Open an issue/proposal. 7-day comment period. Project lead decides.
- Objection: Any Council member can escalate to Category C.

### Category C: Significant (Council decision)
- Architecture changes, new projects, deprecating major features
- Process: Written proposal. 14-day comment period. Council vote (simple majority).
- Objection: Any Board member can request Board review.

### Category D: Organizational (Board decision)
- Budget, partnerships, legal matters, charter amendments
- Process: Board proposal. 30-day comment period. Board vote (2/3 majority).

## 3. Proposal Process

All proposals follow this format:

```markdown
## Proposal: [Title]
**Category:** [A/B/C/D]
**Author:** [Name]
**Date:** [YYYY-MM-DD]

### Summary
[One paragraph]

### Motivation
[Why does this matter?]

### Details
[What specifically is being proposed?]

### Impact
[Who/what does this affect?]

### Alternatives Considered
[What else did you consider?]
```

Proposals are submitted as GitHub issues with the `proposal` label.

## 4. Voting

When a vote is required:

- **Council votes:** 7-day voting window. Simple majority passes. Quorum is 50% of Council members.
- **Board votes:** 14-day voting window. 2/3 majority passes. Quorum is 2/3 of Board members.
- **Community polls:** Non-binding. Used to gauge sentiment. Open for 7 days.

Votes are cast publicly on the relevant GitHub issue. No secret ballots — transparency is non-negotiable.

## 5. Conflict Resolution

### Level 1: Direct Conversation
The parties involved talk it out. Preferably in a public channel so others can learn from the resolution.

### Level 2: Council Mediation
If direct conversation fails, a Council member not involved in the conflict mediates.

### Level 3: Board Arbitration
If Council mediation fails, the Board makes a binding decision. Board decisions in conflicts are final.

### Level 4: Fork
If a contributor fundamentally disagrees with the Foundation's direction, they are free to fork. We believe in open source — forking is a feature, not a failure. The fork retains full rights to all open-licensed materials.

## 6. Transparency Requirements

- All proposals, discussions, and votes are public
- Meeting notes are published within 48 hours
- Financial reports are published quarterly
- No private decision-making channels for Foundation business

Exceptions: Personnel matters, legal issues under attorney-client privilege, and security vulnerabilities before patch.

## 7. Roles and Term Limits

- **Board members:** 2-year terms, renewable once, then 1-year cooling off
- **Council members:** No term limits, but annual confirmation vote (simple majority of contributors)
- **Project leads:** Appointed by Council, can be removed by Council vote
- **Contributors:** No limits — contribute as much or as little as you want

## 8. Amendment Process

These bylaws may be amended by:

1. A proposal from any contributor (Category C process)
2. 14-day public comment period
3. Approval by simple majority of Council AND simple majority of Board

---

*Good governance serves the mission, not the other way around. If these bylaws ever get in the way of doing the right thing, change them.*
